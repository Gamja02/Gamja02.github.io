#MobileApp #UXUI

The Pain Point

Planning day trips to be a bit of a hassle. We get it – the current process can be a real buzzkill.

Current approaches to planning day trips often lack efficiency, leading to a time-consuming and frustrating process, particularly when individuals are uncertain about destinations, timings, and transportation. The effort required to gather all this information and plan accordingly can quickly become exhausting.

Solution

Don’t spend too much time on planning. Let Weekend Shortcut curate your perfect day trip, from destinations to schedules, so you can focus on making memories, not itineraries.

I addressed the challenge of inefficient day trip planning by creating an app that offers personalized recommendations based on user preferences. The app streamlines the process by generating comprehensive schedules, complete with departure times, transportation durations, and optimal time allocations at each destination.

Explore

For those who has no idea where to go, what to do. The app suggests places and events based on the user’s interest, mood, and popularity.

Schedule

The app provides a full schedule based on the user’s preferred departure time, arrival time, and time spent at the spot.

Ideation

Why the task fail?

There are more than one pain point when Stephanie tries to plan and go for a weekend getaway with her fellow interns. One of them is a lack of time to plan things out and communicate. From here, I decided to help them save time by providing recommendations and a schedule.

Workflow

The user journey is simple. Once the user chooses a recommended place or event, they will be prompted with several questions that will eventually help build a personalized schedule.

What's Next

Started as a side project during the internship, but has more potential.

Weekend Shortcut started as a side project while I was taking the UX/UI Pathway during my internship. However, the concept has the potential to address various pain points beyond the ones previously described. For instance, it could be adapted as a conference or event app, offering recommendations and a comprehensive schedule for the day. This schedule could include event durations, estimated walking times, and start and end times. I am considering creating additional mockups tailored for these purposes to further explore the application of Weekend Shortcut.
